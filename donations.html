Frequently Asked Questions

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes, all donations to Genricycle are tax-deductible. You will receive a tax receipt for your contribution.

How is my donation used?

Your donations directly support our recycling initiatives, subsidize medicine costs for those in need, and help develop sustainable healthcare solutions.

Can I make a donation in someone's honor?

Yes, you can make a donation in honor or memory of someone. Simply include this information in the message field.

How can I donate medicines?

You can donate medicines through our online form. We accept unopened medicines that are not expired. For partially used medicines, our team will evaluate them before acceptance. All donated medicines undergo quality checks before redistribution.

What are the requirements for blood donation?

Blood donors must be at least 18 years old, weigh at least 50kg, and be in good health. You should not have donated blood in the last 3 months. After submitting the form, our medical team will contact you to schedule an appointment at our partner blood bank.

How does organ/body part donation work?

Organ donation is a serious decision. After submitting your interest through our form, a medical professional will contact you to discuss the process, eligibility, and legal requirements. We facilitate both living donations (kidney, partial liver, bone marrow) and after-death donations through proper medical and legal channels.

Is there any compensation for blood or organ donation?

No, all blood and organ donations are voluntary and without financial compensation, as per medical ethics and Indian law. However, donors may receive free health check-ups and screening as part of the donation process.
