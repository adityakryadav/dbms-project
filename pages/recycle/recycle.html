Frequently Asked Questions

We accept non-hazardous medical waste including plastic containers, glass vials, cardboard packaging, and certain types of sharps in approved containers. Hazardous waste, infectious materials, and chemotherapy waste require special handling and may not be eligible.

Our process involves collection, sorting, sterilization (when required), and processing into recyclable materials. Different materials follow different recycling paths - plastics are processed into pellets, glass is crushed for reuse, and paper/cardboard is pulped for recycling.

For registered hospitals and labs, basic recycling services are provided at minimal cost, with potential for earning rewards points. Pricing depends on volume, frequency, and waste type. Contact us for a customized quote.

All waste must be properly segregated, contained in appropriate containers, and labeled according to waste type. Our team provides training and guidelines for proper preparation. Improperly prepared waste may be rejected at pickup.

Pickup frequency depends on your facility's needs and waste volume. Options range from daily to monthly scheduled pickups, with emergency pickups available when needed. Regular schedules help optimize our routes and reduce carbon emissions.
